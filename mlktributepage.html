Martin Luther King Jr.

The man who spoke for change

Martin Luther King Jr. was an advocate for equal rights, leading protests and marches for his people

Here are some of his accomplishments listed

  • 1955 - He led the Montgomery Bus Boycott which lasted for 385 days
  • 1957 - He helped found and became first president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC)
  • 1963 - He started a movement called the Burmingham Campaign in a town that was extremely racially divided
  • 1963 - He was instrumental in organizing The Great March on Washington
  • 1963 - In late 1963, he delivered his famous "I Have A Dream Speech," inspiring the Civil Rights Movement further
  • 1963 - Time Magazine featured him as "Man Of The Year"
  • 1964 - He greatly influenced the passing of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and Voting Rights Act of 1965
  • 1964 - He became the youngest person at the time to receive the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1968 - He was assassinated, leaving behind the legacy of pursuing Civil Rights
  • 1977 - Awarded the Presidental Medal of Freedom posthumously
  • 2004 - Awarded the Congressional Gold Medal posthumously

"I have a dream that one day this nation will rise up and live out the true meaning of its creed: 'We hold these truths to be self-evident: that all men are created equal.'"

Martin Luther King Jr. in 1963 at Washington D.C.
